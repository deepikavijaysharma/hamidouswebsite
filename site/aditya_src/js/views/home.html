TECH TALENT FEATURE: It’s All in the Family - Elite Athletes
Giving Back: Oracle d.Tech Coach Inspires Teenage Girls

Our “Tech Talent” feature for October is Dustin Minter, Cloud Customer Success Manager, Public Sector/Enterprise. Dustin has some mighty fine DNA. His grandpa, Harry Minter, played for the Philadelphia Phillies baseball team, his father, Mike Minter, was on the NBA D-League team for the Dallas Mavericks, Dustin reached the Final Four of the Elite Eight Basketball tournament at Tarleton State University 2003-2006, his middle brother, Kyle, played soccer at the University of Memphis, and his youngest brother, Chase, is playing soccer in the MLS for Real Monarchs Salt Lake City. Dustin’s talents are a great addition to the Oracle Solution Engineering team as he brings to bear his winning and competitive legacy to help our customers succeed in the cloud.

Bella Dosovitsky volunteered for a two-week d.Tech high school inter-session through the Oracle Education Foundation. She coached a 3D printing class of 25 girls, who went through explorative learning of the 3D Printing industry, the fabrication process, and turning their ideas into real-life designs using CAD software and 3D Printers.

Bella also got to present to the students our personal and professional journeys, lessons learned, and informal conversations about: self-esteem, women in technology, personal development, juggling our desires with our parents’ pressure, and choosing a profession.

Why Give Back? – Bella emphatically said that it was “Surely, the single most inspiring things I’ve done at Oracle so far.”
