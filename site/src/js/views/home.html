Design a Cloud Solution

Category : Opportunity Execution

Description

This Solution-Engineering-as-a-Service includes:
  • Collaboratively working with a customer to turn their Cloud Vision into a robust Solution Presentation and Oracle Sales Proposal.
  • Producing a sales proposal, including developing logical and physical architectures, sizing current and future workloads, recommending phased implementation roadmaps, creating the intake provisioning document, solution presentation, and contributing to the Oracle Sales Proposal.
  • This service typically uses ECAL [hyperlink] Stages 2-4 as the underlying execution framework.

SE Role Engagement

Roles
  • ECAs lead this engagement and are responsible for the majority of the deliverables.
  • Platform Specialists and Hub SEs work on Portfolio and Workload Analysis efforts, contribute to the physical architecture designs, and provide foundation services as needed (e.g. Customer Discovery of current state IT Portfolio, Customer Demos, Sizing/Quoting/Config).
  • This service may spawn the “Build a POC” [hyperlink] service.
  • Business Value Consultants support any value-based activities and deliverables, which can also fall under the “Build a Business Case” service.
  • CSMs are responsible for ECAL Migration and Adoption Planning activities and deliverables, and the subsequent “Adopt a Cloud Solution” service.
  • For more information, see our ECAL [hyperlink] website.

Customer Discovery

Category: Foundational SE Services

Description

This Solution-Engineering-as-a-Service includes:
  • Collaboratively working with a customer to analyze specific business/technical use cases and associated opportunities and challenges.
  • This process is highly consultative, focusing on the customer’s business/technical goals as well as existing processes, technologies, systems, personnel and business environment.
  • This builds valuable customer trust, but also uniquely positions Oracle to business-justify and cost-justify immediate implementation of a solution – specifically immediate implementation of an Oracle Cloud solution.
  • Deliverables include a presentation outlining use cases, goals, challenges, prescription analyses and benefits. Deliverables also include a solution roadmap and Oracle sales proposal.

SE Role Engagement

Roles
  • ECAs lead initial steps in this engagement and are responsible for the majority of the early deliverables.
  • Platform Specialists from the GEO Field support all phases closely, coordinating customer events and providing seamless handoff to solution and proposal.

Chip Baber, Solution Engineer, although a very humble guy, could do some “braggin” about his 17-year career at Oracle, as he has been a great innovator for the corporation.

  • 1. What does innovation mean to you?
    Innovation is the courage to respectfully show others a new way to experience the world. Its' success lies in the eyes of the viewer, not the solution created. True Innovation speaks to the viewer emotionally first, then carries the emotional reaction through the message it serves to convey.

  • 2. What are some of the innovations that you have contributed to for Solution Engineering?
    The Cloud Accelerate program is by far the most innovative program I have had the opportunity to participate in at Oracle. It is truly a new way to speak, show, and engage our customers. A close second, is the creation of the Online Interactive Lab Guide framework for all our OPC workshops. It pulls customers into our OPC technical content inside Hands on Labs. Programmatically, I had a chance to create the demonstrations and build the companies first public parallax based web experience in an experiment with marketing during 2014. Last but not least, I had the opportunity to be the lead solution engineer on the original Marvel/Oracle Ironman partnership. We created a 3-part animated series showing Oracle software inside the world of Marvel.

  • 3. What are some of the new innovations that you will be bringing to SE this year?
    In FY18, our team is squarely focused on re-engaging the developer community. We are looking to create new engagement channels, innovative assets, and find ways to emotional capture the ever- elusive eyes, ears, and hearts of developers.
    Technically speaking, if I could build anything in the coming year, it would be a Cloud Native Blockchain Development experience. Specifically, I would introduce and demystify the world of autonomous contracts to our user base and development community, then provide them a path inside our PaaS offering to foster future development.

  • 4. Who are some of your favorite innovators? Why?
    The inventors of Draft Kings (Jason Robins, Matthew Kalish, and Paul Liberman) are at the top of my list. These guys, basically, invented a way to transform and monetize the world of fantasy sports. The way they took a human experience that existed for 10+ years and found a way to leverage technology to enhance, extend, and most importantly make profitable the experience. This is an example of innovation in its' purest form.
